- Core claim: Center-of-Pressure tactile representation preserves useful dense contact information while remaining robust enough for zero-shot sim-to-real dexterous manipulation.
- Methodology: CoP is paired with differentiable-dynamics sensor calibration and evaluated on blind peg-in-hole insertion and ball balancing using a multi-fingered hand.
- Most important finding: CoP-conditioned policies achieved zero-shot sim-to-real transfer across both tasks and outperformed binary-contact and raw-taxel baselines.
- Limitation: The abstract reports only two manipulation tasks, so generalization beyond those contact-rich settings is not established.
